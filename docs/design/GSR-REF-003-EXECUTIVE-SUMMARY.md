**Purpose**

This document provides the Longview Solutions Group command team with a

**The Problem We Are Solving**

The disaster response workforce in the United States lacks a unified,
verifiable professional credentialing system. Responders currently rely
on fragmented records, self-reported experience, and informal reputation
to demonstrate their qualifications. Training certificates are scattered
across filing cabinets and email inboxes. Deployment history is
undocumented or unverifiable. There is no portable, career-long
professional identity that follows a responder across incidents,
agencies, and jurisdictions.

This creates real consequences: agencies cannot efficiently verify
responder qualifications during mobilization, employers rely on resumes
and word-of-mouth rather than documented evidence, responders have no
structured pathway for professional advancement, and the profession as a
whole lacks the credibility infrastructure that exists in virtually
every other high-consequence field.

**The Solution**

Grey Sky Responders is a web and mobile platform that professionalizes
disaster response through verified, career-long professional
development. Every certification and credential on the platform is
anchored to FEMA RTLT (Responder Training and Leadership Toolkit)
standards as the minimum threshold. Grey Sky does not replace FEMA or
government qualification systems --- it builds on them, translating
public-sector standards into a portable, professional identity that
follows the responder throughout their career.

The platform is built as a Progressive Web App (PWA) --- a single
codebase that works in browsers and installs on mobile devices, with
offline access for field conditions. It is designed to support a base of
100,000 members with the ability to scale during disaster-driven traffic
surges.

**Core Platform Capabilities**

**Responder Profile and Dashboard**

Each responder maintains a verified professional profile that serves as
their operational hub. The dashboard displays certification progress
against RTLT pathways, deployment records with verification status,
pending validation and evaluation requests, a complete document library,
professional connections, and structured views against NQS mission areas
or agency-specific requirements. All data is exportable as professional
reports suitable for agency credentialing boards, employers, and
procurement reviews.

**Deployment Records and Three-Tier Verification**

Responders document their deployment history on the platform. Each
deployment record captures the incident, dates, position held,
organization, complexity level, and jurisdiction. Records exist at one
of three verification tiers:

- **Self-Certified.** The responder entered the record with no external
  validation. Displayed with clear designation.

- **Validated (360 Approach).** The responder sends a validation request
  to anyone willing to attest. The validator sees the record and
  responds Yes or No. A Yes response requires acknowledgment of a legal
  attestation notice. No narrative is required.

- **Evaluated (ICS 225 Approach).** The responder sends an evaluation
  request. The evaluator completes a simple form --- five performance
  areas rated 1 through 5, modeled on the FEMA ICS 225. Written
  commentary is required only if the evaluator selects 1 or 5 on any
  area. The same legal attestation applies.

Both validation and evaluation responses are immutable once submitted
and stored in an auditable record.

**Certification Pathways and Credentialing**

Role-based certification tracks aligned to FEMA RTLT standards with
progressive tiers reflecting experience, complexity, and leadership.
Certification decisions are based on documented, verified evidence. The
verification tier of deployment records directly influences what
certification levels a responder can pursue. Credentials are issued
digitally and verifiable via unique URL or QR code.

**Professional Networking**

Members can search and discover other responders by affinity, position,
location, agency, or disaster experience. The platform supports
professional connections, direct messaging, and team grouping based on
shared incident history. This is professional networking --- not social
media.

**Incident Stories**

Responders can attach structured professional reflections to their
deployment records --- capturing what happened, what they learned, and
what the next generation should know. Stories build an organic knowledge
base for the community while adding qualitative depth to the responder's
professional record.

**Affinities, Positions, Locations, and Organizations**

Responder profiles capture hazard types, functional specialties, sector
experience, team affiliations, NIMS/ICS position qualifications and
history, geographic location and deployment availability, and
organizational affiliations --- all drawn from controlled vocabularies
aligned to FEMA standards.

**Training Connection**

Phase I provides a curated training catalog connecting responders to
available courses --- local, state, federal, and private --- mapped
directly to certification pathway gaps. Grey Sky aggregates demand to
broker centralized course delivery in locations that serve regional
needs. Phase II introduces Grey Sky-developed continuing education and
advanced applied training content.

**AI-Powered Document Processing**

Integration with the Anthropic API (Claude) provides automated
extraction of structured data from uploaded certificates and training
records, cross-referencing against RTLT requirements, gap analysis
guidance for responders, workforce readiness reporting for agencies, and
integrity monitoring across validation patterns. This reduces the
verification workload dramatically and enables the platform to scale
without proportional staffing increases.

**Public Access**

The public-facing website explains Grey Sky's credentialing framework
and drives membership. A credential verification portal allows anyone
with a responder's direct link or QR code to verify their credential
status in real time. Responders may opt in to a limited public profile
viewable via their direct link, but there is no public search directory.
Public data visualizations include aggregate responder density maps (no
deeper than zip code), participation depth across team types and
specializations, and incident and disaster timelines showing Grey Sky's
operational footprint.

**Sky Points Economy**

Grey Sky uses an internal currency called Sky Points to drive platform
engagement and monetize transactional features. Responders purchase Sky
Points in bundles and spend them on platform actions.

  ----------------------------------------- -----------------------------
  **Action**                                **Sky Points Cost**

  Send a Validation Request                 10 Sky Points (\$1.00)

  Send an Evaluation Request                20 Sky Points (\$2.00)

  Certification Pathway Application         5,000 Sky Points (\$500)

  Credential Issuance                       Priced per credential type

  Credential Renewal                        Priced per credential type
  ----------------------------------------- -----------------------------

Annual membership (\$100) includes 100 Sky Points, enabling every new
member to take immediate action --- validating deployment records or
initiating evaluations --- from day one. The Sky Points model provides
prepaid revenue, pricing flexibility, promotional capability, and a
natural engagement incentive through unspent balances.

**Technology and Infrastructure**

Grey Sky will be hosted on Microsoft Azure, leveraging Longview's
existing Microsoft 365 tenant. Azure was selected for its government and
public-sector alignment --- critical for future agency contracts --- and
its integration with the Microsoft ecosystem already in use.

The platform is built as a Progressive Web App using Next.js (frontend),
a containerized backend on Azure Container Apps, Azure Database for
PostgreSQL, Azure Blob Storage for documents, Stripe for payment
processing, and the Anthropic API for AI-powered document analysis. The
architecture auto-scales to handle disaster-driven traffic surges of
10--50x normal volume.

Estimated infrastructure costs are \$1,000--\$3,000 per month during
early operations, scaling to \$8,000--\$20,000 per month at 100,000
